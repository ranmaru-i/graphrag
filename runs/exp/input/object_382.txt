that are, well, fair enough.\n\nA clumsy example: I read a great book, then I meet you for breakfast, where I tell you about the story and quote a couple of my favorite passages to you.\n\nTo suggest I’ve just committed copyright infringement wouldn’t only be silly – it would mean that copyright protections were getting in the way of the commercial furtherment of the author/rightsholder (i.e. you might go and buy the book on my recommendation).\n\nGoogle’s submission to the USCO contains a number of passages that posit a similar position… but for generative AI models.
